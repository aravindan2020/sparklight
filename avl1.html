AVL Tree

1. Abbreviation of AVL

AVL stands for Adelson-Velsky and Landis, the two Russian scientists who introduced this self-balancing binary search tree in 1962.

2. Definition of AVL Tree

An AVL Tree is a self-balancing Binary Search Tree where the difference between heights of left and right subtrees (Balance Factor) of every node is at most -1, 0, or +1.

Balance Factor = Height(Left Subtree) − Height(Right Subtree)

Why AVL is Powerful? (1 Million Videos Example)

Imagine 1,000,000 videos stored. If stored in a normal BST (worst case), Height could become = 1,000,000 Searching time becomes very slow. But in AVL Tree: Height ≈ log₂(1,000,000) ≈ 20 That means even with 1 million records, maximum search depth is only 20 levels! This is why AVL gives O(log n) search time.

3. Types of Rotations in AVL Tree

LL Rotation (Left-Left Rotation)

When it occurs:
When insertion happens in the left subtree of the left child.

Problem Structure:

      30
     /
   20
  /
10

To fix this imbalance, a Right Rotation is performed. The left child becomes the new root.

RR Rotation (Right-Right Rotation)

When it occurs:
When insertion happens in the right subtree of the right child.

Problem Structure:

10
  \
   20
     \
      30

To fix this imbalance, a Left Rotation is performed. The right child becomes the new root.

LR Rotation (Left-Right Rotation)

When it occurs:
When insertion happens in the right subtree of the left child.

Problem Structure:

      30
     /
   10
     \
      20

This is a double rotation:
1. Left Rotation on left child
2. Right Rotation on root

RL Rotation (Right-Left Rotation)

When it occurs:
When insertion happens in the left subtree of the right child.

Problem Structure:

10
  \
   30
  /
20

This is also a double rotation:
1. Right Rotation on right child
2. Left Rotation on root

4. Applications of AVL Tree in Biotechnology

  • Fast searching of DNA sequences in genomic databases
  • Protein structure indexing systems
  • Gene mutation lookup systems
  • Clinical data record management
  • Bioinformatics database indexing
  • Efficient storage of large biological datasets
  • Searching drug compound information quickly

In biotechnology, millions of biological records are stored. AVL ensures fast retrieval in logarithmic time.
