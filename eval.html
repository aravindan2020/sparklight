Evaluation of Postfix Expression

1. Why Evaluation of Postfix Expression?

  • Postfix expressions eliminate the need for parentheses.
  • Operator precedence is implicitly handled.
  • Stack-based evaluation is simple and efficient.
  • Widely used in expression evaluation and compilers.

2. Algorithm

  1. Initialize an empty stack.
  2. Scan the postfix expression from left to right.
  3. If an operand is encountered, push it onto the stack.
  4. If an operator is encountered:
    • Pop the top element as A.
    • Pop the next top element as B.
    • Compute B operator A.
    • Push the result back onto the stack.
  5. After scanning all symbols, the top of the stack is the final result.

3. Examples (Long Expressions – Illustrated)

Example 1: Postfix = 23*54*+9-

SymbolOperationStack
2Push2
3Push2, 3
*Pop A=3, B=2 → 2×3=6 → Push6
5Push6, 5
4Push6, 5, 4
*Pop A=4, B=5 → 5×4=20 → Push6, 20
+Pop A=20, B=6 → 6+20=26 → Push26
9Push26, 9
-Pop A=9, B=26 → 26−9=17 → Push17

Result: 17
