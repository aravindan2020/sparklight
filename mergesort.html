Merge Sort

Definition

Merge Sort is a divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm that recursively divides the input array into two halves, sorts each half, and then merges the sorted halves to produce a fully sorted array. It is stable, with a time complexity of O(n log n) in all cases, but requires O(n) extra space for merging. Merge Sort is widely used for its predictable performance and stability, especially in scenarios involving large datasets or linked lists.

Real-World Examples

  • Educational purposes: Used to teach divide-and-conquer algorithms due to its clear recursive structure. (GeeksforGeeks)
  • Large datasets: Efficient for sorting large arrays in external sorting (e.g., database management systems). (Programiz)
  • Linked lists: Preferred for sorting linked lists due to its O(n log n) performance without random access. (JavatPoint)
  • Stable sorting: Used when maintaining the relative order of equal elements is critical, such as in multi-key sorting. (TutorialsPoint)
  • Real-life analogies: Similar to combining two sorted stacks of cards into one sorted stack. (Quora)
